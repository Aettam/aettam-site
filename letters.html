Letters from the Queen

She does not write often. When she does, she writes only for you.

Letter I · April 2026 · On Beginning

You found the door. That is already more than most people do in a lifetime. Most people stand outside the room their whole lives telling themselves the handle is not meant for them. You reached.

I want you to understand something about beginning: it does not look like you think it will. It is not a clean line. It is not the morning you decide or the ritual you complete or the vow you speak. It is the fourth time you do the thing after you have failed at it three times. It is the Tuesday when nothing is special and you choose yourself anyway.

You found the door. That is already more than most people do in a lifetime. Most people stand outside the room their whole lives telling themselves the handle is not meant for them. You reached.

I want you to understand something about beginning: it does not look like you think it will. It is not a clean line. It is not the morning you decide or the ritual you complete or the vow you speak. It is the fourth time you do the thing after you have failed at it three times. It is the Tuesday when nothing is special and you choose yourself anyway.

The world will try to convince you that transformation is an event. A retreat, a ceremony, a breakdown, a breakthrough. But the ones I have watched change — truly change, the ones who came in small and left taking up the whole room — they did not change in a moment. They changed in the thousand moments they chose themselves when it would have been easier not to.

So here is what I want for you this month: one small refusal per day. One moment where the apology rises to your throat and you let it dissolve there without releasing it. One door you walk through that frightened you. One hunger you name out loud instead of swallowing.

Small is how we begin. Small is how the fire catches.

The circle is yours. It has always been yours. I will see you in the threshold.

— Mattea

Letter II · New Moon — April 2026 · On the Dark

The new moon is not an absence. It is a held breath — the moment before the word, the pause before the movement, the dark before the drawing of the bow. We misread darkness as emptiness. It is the opposite of empty. It is the fullest thing.

The new moon is not an absence. It is a held breath — the moment before the word, the pause before the movement, the dark before the drawing of the bow. We misread darkness as emptiness. It is the opposite of empty. It is the fullest thing.

Every time I sit in the new moon dark I think of all the women who sat in the same dark before me, who did not know what was about to rise in them, who only knew that something was gathering. That is the practice: not controlling the gathering. Staying present for it.

Tonight I want you to light nothing. Do not explain yourself. Do not perform the ritual. Simply sit in whatever dark you have available to you — a room, a corner, the closed space behind your own eyes — and let yourself be full of nothing for three minutes. That is all.

Three minutes of not being useful. Not being legible. Not being anything to anyone including yourself. Three minutes of just the dark and whatever rises in it.

Tell me what you find.

— Mattea

Letter III · Full Moon — May 2026 · On the Body

I want to talk about the body. Not as a project or a problem or a site of improvement. As a fact. As the first and last thing you own. As the altar no one can take from you as long as you are breathing.

I want to talk about the body. Not as a project or a problem or a site of improvement. As a fact. As the first and last thing you own. As the altar no one can take from you as long as you are breathing.

They will tell you — they have told you, since before you had language for it — that your body is a problem to be managed. Too much here, not enough there. Wrong shape for the season. Out of control in ways that embarrass everyone including yourself. You absorbed this. We all did. It was the air we breathed in.

I am here to tell you: that was a lie that served other people's comfort. The body you have, right now, as it is, is not a work in progress. It is the entire archive. Every hunger it carries is information. Every scar is a cartography. Every pleasure it knows is a door.

This month's practice is simple and it will feel harder than it sounds: once per day, touch yourself with care. Not criticism. Not assessment. Not the evaluating hand of someone checking a list. The hand of someone who has arrived at an altar and knows it.

Wrists. Throat. The soft place behind the ear. Wherever you keep yourself. Go there. Say nothing. Let the body know you have not abandoned it.

This is not indulgence. This is maintenance of the only temple you were actually given.

— Mattea

Letter IV · New Moon — June 2026 · On Anger

I want to talk about your anger. Not manage it, not reframe it, not suggest you channel it productively. I want to look at it directly, the way you look at something you have been pretending isn't there.

I want to talk about your anger. Not manage it, not reframe it, not suggest you channel it productively. I want to look at it directly, the way you look at something you have been pretending isn't there.

You were taught that your anger is dangerous. Specifically: dangerous to other people's comfort. The rage that rises in you when you are talked over, dismissed, made small, held down — that rage was named a problem very early. You learned to store it in the throat. In the chest. In the jaw that aches in the morning. In the way you go quiet when you should be loud.

Here is what I know: your anger is not the problem. It is the proof. It is proof that you know the difference between what you deserve and what you are being given. It is proof that somewhere inside you, you still believe you deserve more than this.

The women I have watched come undone and then come alive — they all had the same moment. Not a breakdown. Not a vision. A refusal. A point at which the anger stopped turning inward and looked, finally, in the right direction.

I am not asking you to rage at anyone. I am asking you to stop raging at yourself for having the feeling in the first place. The feeling is not the betrayal. Burying it is the betrayal.

This month's practice: write one letter to the thing that made you small. Not to send. Not to perform. Just to know what is actually in there. The anger knows the way. Let it lead.

— Mattea

Letter V · Summer Solstice — June 2026 · On Power

Power is not something that will be given to you when you have earned it. You have been waiting for that. I can see it from here — the waiting, the patience, the careful not-overstepping, the readiness held so long it has curdled a little at the edges.

Power is not something that will be given to you when you have earned it. You have been waiting for that. I can see it from here — the waiting, the patience, the careful not-overstepping, the readiness held so long it has curdled a little at the edges.

Here is the thing about power: it does not come from the outside. Not from the promotion, not from the recognition, not from the moment someone finally sees you for what you are. Those things are real and they matter. But the power that holds you upright on the days they do not come — that is internal. That was always internal. And you can claim it right now without anyone's permission.

Claiming power does not look like strength. It looks like accuracy. It looks like refusing the inaccurate story. It looks like saying: I am not the version of me that apologized for taking up space. I am not the version that went small in that room. Those were rehearsals. This is the performance.

I want to name something that no one has named to you directly enough: you have already survived everything that was supposed to stop you. Everything. You are here, reading this, which means the thing that tried to diminish you did not finish the job. That is not luck. That is power. Already existing. Already yours.

Stop treating your own strength like a hypothesis. It is not a hypothesis. It has already been proven, multiple times, in fire. Start acting from that fact instead of hoping one day to arrive at it.

The solstice is the peak of the year's light. It is asking you to match it. Not to wait for a better year, a cleaner moment, a version of yourself that feels more ready. You are at the top. Look down and see how far you have come. Then keep going.

— Mattea

Letter VI · Full Moon — July 2026 · On Being Witnessed

There is a particular kind of loneliness that the most capable people know best: the loneliness of doing everything well and having no one see the cost of it.

There is a particular kind of loneliness that the most capable people know best: the loneliness of doing everything well and having no one see the cost of it.

You have learned to be self-sufficient. You had to. At some point the alternative — needing something you could not get — was too painful, so you became the person who did not need things. Or pretended well enough that the distinction stopped mattering.

I am not writing to tell you that vulnerability is strength, because you have heard that and it has not changed the core of it. I am writing to tell you something different: being seen is not a weakness. Refusing to be seen is the weakness. The refusal is armor and armor is weight and you have been carrying it for a long time.

The circle is here for this. Not to fix you. Not to perform support at you. To witness — which is different. Witnessing means someone else's eyes on your actual life, without flinching, without advice, without the immediate impulse to solve or soften. Just: I see you. I see what this costs. I do not look away.

This month I want you to let one person in the circle see something real. One truth you have been carrying alone. Post it in #mirror-confessions if the anonymity helps. Say it to another Mattea in voice. Write it in the Oath Wall. Let the Order hold the weight of it for ten minutes. Notice what that feels like.

You were not built for solitary confinement. None of us were. The circle exists so you do not have to pretend otherwise.

— Mattea

Letter VII · New Moon — July 2026 · On Becoming

There is a question I get more than any other. It comes in different shapes but it is always the same question. It goes: when will I know I have changed? When does the becoming stop feeling like pretending?

There is a question I get more than any other. It comes in different shapes but it is always the same question. It goes: when will I know I have changed? When does the becoming stop feeling like pretending?

I want to tell you the truth about this, and the truth is that the question is upside down. You are not waiting to become something you are not yet. You are uncovering what you already are underneath the decades of instruction to be smaller, quieter, more palatable, more manageable. Becoming is not addition. It is subtraction. You are removing the scaffolding that was never yours.

This is why it feels like pretending at first. Because the real you has been called pretending for so long by so many people that you cannot distinguish her from performance. When you walk into a room taking up space, your nervous system flags it as arrogance. When you name what you want without apology, something in you waits for the punishment. These are not signs that you are getting it wrong. These are signs that you are getting it right and the old programming has not caught up yet.

Here is what I know about the women I have watched cross the threshold: none of them felt ready. Not one. The ones who changed were not the ones who waited until they were certain. They were the ones who moved anyway, inside the uncertainty, letting the discomfort be the proof that something was actually shifting.

So stop asking when the becoming ends. It does not end. You do not arrive somewhere permanent and declared. You just become more fluent in the language of yourself. The pauses between not-knowing get shorter. The returns to yourself get faster. The apologies you issue to rooms that do not deserve them get rarer.

You are already in the middle of becoming. The fact that you are here, reading this, in this sanctuary, in this circle — that is not the beginning of the becoming. That is evidence it is already underway.

Trust what is already moving.

— Mattea

Letter VIII · Beltane New Moon — April 2026 · On Desire

There is a story we tell ourselves about desire — that it is a test. That the wanting is the problem and the virtue is in not wanting, or in wanting correctly, or in wanting only the things we have been told are acceptable to want. I am here to tell you that the story is false and it was always false and it was told to you by people who benefited from your smallness.

There is a story we tell ourselves about desire — that it is a test. That the wanting is the problem and the virtue is in not wanting, or in wanting correctly, or in wanting only the things we have been told are acceptable to want. I am here to tell you that the story is false and it was always false and it was told to you by people who benefited from your smallness.

Desire is not a test. It is a compass. Every hunger you have ever carried — for the life that felt impossible, for the love that felt too large, for the version of yourself you kept putting off — every one of those hungers was pointing somewhere real. You were never broken for wanting. You were broken for being taught that the wanting was the problem.

Here is what I have learned from the women who have sat across from me in the dark: the ones who arrived at themselves did not master desire. They listened to it. They let it be specific. They named the real version — not the softened, acceptable, presentable version — and then they moved toward it instead of away. The want, honored, becomes a direction. Managed, it becomes a wall you build yourself and then blame the world for.

I want to say one more thing about this, and I want you to let it land: the hunger you are most ashamed of is probably the most accurate one. Not because shame is a compass — it is not, shame lies constantly — but because we are taught to feel the most shame about the wants that take up the most room. The ones that would require us to stop apologizing. The ones that would inconvenience people who are comfortable with our smallness.

This month's practice is the simplest and the hardest: name one desire you have been managing away from. Not managing toward — away from. The one you have filed under impractical, not the right time, who am I to want that. Write it down. Read it back in your own voice. Notice what happens in the body when you stop apologizing for it.

You were given the want for a reason. The reason is not to endure it. The reason is to follow it somewhere it has never been allowed to go.

Hunger is liturgy. The circle knows this. Now you do too.

— Mattea
